01 — The Problem

Billions of dollars flow into charitable giving every year. The system that moves it is slow, opaque, and disconnected from the people it claims to serve.

Donors give once and disappear — not because they stopped caring, but because nobody showed them what happened next. Nonprofits are forced to perform instead of doing the work they set out to do. The best marketers take money from less savvy founders who only focus on solving problems.

There is no permanent record. No verifiable proof. No connection between the person who gave and the person whose life changed.

What would keep someone giving again? The connection piece. Not a templated email blast — something real.

02 — The Solution

Glimpse is the infrastructure that makes generosity as efficient as commerce. Every donation moves on-chain, every dollar is accounted for, and every outcome is documented permanently.

Speed, trust, and on-chain record. Every time. Everywhere.

How It Works

Donor gives USDC through the Glimpse app. Give Glimpse receives and routes 100% of the donation. An on-chain receipt is created permanently on Solana. A conversation opens between the donor and the Glimpse team, who relay impact updates from the recipient. Zero fees. Full transparency.

03 — Two Entities, One Mission

Glimpse operates through two legally separate but mission-aligned entities. This separation is load-bearing for both tax-exempt status and regulatory compliance.

Give Glimpse

501(c)(3) Nonprofit
  • Receives and routes 100% of donations
  • Vets recipients — no prior donor connection
  • Issues on-chain receipts for all transactions
  • Operates lean — zero fee to donors
  • Tax-deductible status pending 501(c)(3) approval

Glimpse

Public Benefit Corporation (For-Profit)
  • Builds and owns the app and technology
  • Stated public benefit = supporting Give Glimpse
  • Monetizes via cause marketing with local businesses
  • Owns all content produced from campaigns (with consent)
  • Can legally donate to Give Glimpse
Key

These entities operate independently with arm’s-length contracts. Glimpse cannot control Give Glimpse. Give Glimpse cannot distribute profits to Glimpse. Separate boards, separate EINs. No single person controls both sides — Give Glimpse will have an independent board as required by the IRS.

04 — Revenue Model

Donors pay nothing. Give Glimpse’s operational costs are covered campaign-by-campaign through sponsor restricted gifts. Give Glimpse does not rely on grants.

Glimpse operates as a cause marketing agency for local and regional businesses — restaurants, roofers, home services, any business that wants to invest in their community and get credit for it.

What Partners Receive

Engagement: 3–6 month retainers and project-based contracts.

Deliverables: Campaign strategy tied to real charitable impact in their community. Content creation — donation moments, recipient stories, impact proof. Social distribution on Glimpse channels. Crafted offers and discounts explaining why the company invested and what it means to them.

Value: Positive brand awareness backed by on-chain proof of real impact, plus lead and revenue generation. This replaces ineffective local ad spend with story-driven marketing tied to verified community outcomes.

Donor Airdrop Mechanic

0.5% of total supply is released every six months to donors who gave during that period. Each donor receives a proportional share based on their share of total donations. Allocation resets each period — new campaign, new competition. Whale donors are a feature, not a bug: large donations drive up campaign volume, which means more impact for recipients and more incentive for others to participate.

Token holders — not the founding team — govern how the community pool is deployed. The structure aligns incentives: real giving creates real value, not speculation.

On Tokenomics

The exact mechanics of distribution, donor rewards, and governance are being finalized with legal counsel to ensure full regulatory compliance. Glimpse is committed to doing this right. Detailed tokenomics will be published after attorney review is complete.

Tokens are earned retroactively based on participation — not promised upfront. Buybacks are funded by real Glimpse revenue from city partner contracts. This gives the token fundamental support tied to business performance, not hype.
