05 — Questions
What does a real donation look like?
1 Sarah opens Glimpse on her Seeker. She picks "Medical" as her cause and enters $25 USDC.
2 She taps Give. Her wallet signs the transaction in under 2 seconds. Zero fees.
3 $25.00 lands in the Glimpse wallet. A permanent receipt is written to Solana — her wallet, the amount, the timestamp. It can never be changed.
4 A conversation opens in the app. Three days later, Sarah gets a message: her donation helped cover a prescription for a single mom in her area.
5 Sarah gives again.
Where does my money go?
100% of your donation goes forward. Glimpse takes zero platform fees. Your USDC goes directly to the Glimpse wallet, which routes it to verified recipients identified by our community partners. Every transaction is recorded permanently on Solana.
Who decides who gets the money?
Glimpse works with community partners — nonprofits, churches, local organizations — who already know the needs on the ground. They identify and verify recipients. Glimpse handles the funds and the proof.
Is this tax-deductible?
No. Glimpse is a Public Benefit Corporation, not a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Your donation is a direct gift from one person to another. We are in the process of applying for the appropriate nonprofit status, but as of today, donations are not tax-deductible.
How do I know my donation was received?
Every donation creates a permanent transaction on Solana's public ledger. You can verify it yourself on Solana Explorer. The receipt includes your wallet address, the exact amount, and a timestamp — it can never be changed or deleted.
Do I need a Solana Seeker?
Yes. Glimpse is built exclusively for the Solana Seeker — a mobile device designed for crypto. You'll need a Seeker with a wallet and USDC to donate.
What is Glimpse, exactly?
Glimpse is a zero-fee donation app. You give USDC. It's recorded permanently on Solana. A conversation opens so you can see exactly what your money did. That's it. No middlemen. No mystery. Just a direct line between you and the person whose life changed.
